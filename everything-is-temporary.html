Everything is Temporary

I like the concept of impermanence. I first had any inkling of what it means when I was around 7 or 8. My mother threw away my 2 or 3 years old stamp collection as garbage. I remember that day very well. I had never been more crushed, my cherished stamps and hard work at exchanging stamps with friends gone down the drain. I am not sure if that prepared me for the coming years of impermanence but it was the beginning of the knowledge that the world as a stable entity was a myth.

Growing old brings another realisation that most adults actually do not know what the right thing to do is. Even worse, there is no right thing to do, only what society judges as right. The right thing to do these days, seems to be to make sure everything is permanent, always there, forever. A lot of people think the Web Platform is the right technology of the future because it has ‘always been there’. Permanence and stability are features that people want, yet do not find any use for.

There is great respect for people who are old and not dead, or things that have existed for several centuries. There is a mythical belief that just because something exists for a long time, it must be good or valuable.

The option of accepting transience of actions simply does not exist any more. Nobody considers it appropriate for you to delete code you wrote. They encourage you to mark it abandonded, or transfer it to someone else. If you do, then you are called out for ‘fucking your users’ or schooled on the responsibility we have to projects we create.

Nothing makes me more upset than finding something I love no longer exists. But it makes me question, learn, and create. Impermanence endures and accepting it so gives me space to let go, find new ways of living.

I have attempted my subversion of this want for stability by mostly just restricting my interactions online to Twitter (which seems to be designed to guarantee I wont be able to find a tweet I made yesterday), and by now deleting as much of my code on Github.

Maybe it is not right, but it is necessary.
